Dosa

Dosa is a popular South Indian dish that resembles a thin, crispy crepe made from fermented rice and black lentils batter. It has a delightful golden-brown exterior and a slightly tangy flavor due to the fermentation process. Dosas are incredibly versatile and can be enjoyed plain or with a variety of fillings, the most popular being the spiced potato filling, known as masala dosa. Traditionally served with sambar and coconut chutney, dosa is a beloved breakfast item but is also enjoyed throughout the day as a snack or meal.

Ingredients

  • Rice - 2 cups
  • Urad Dal (Black Gram Lentils) - 1/2 cup
  • Fenugreek Seeds - 1/2 teaspoon
  • Salt - to taste
  • Water - as needed for soaking and grinding
  • Oil or Ghee - for cooking the dosas (as needed)

Steps

  1. Soak 2 cups of rice, 1/2 cup of urad dal, and 1/2 teaspoon of fenugreek seeds in water separately for 4-6 hours.
  2. Grind the soaked rice and urad dal separately into smooth pastes, then mix them together in a bowl.
  3. Cover and let the batter ferment in a warm place for 8-12 hours or overnight until it rises.
  4. Add salt to the fermented batter and mix well.
  5. Heat a greased skillet, pour and spread a ladleful of batter into a thin circle, drizzle with oil, and cook until golden brown.
  6. Fold and remove from skillet, serving hot with coconut chutney and sambar.
